## General Notes
- **Jack** was recently discharged from the hospital and could only train in a very limited capacity, so he is absent from most of these notes.
- **Guest trainer Brent** opened the session leading dropdowns; Cody then moved the group through hip tosses and arm drags. Kytel and Brent demonstrated proper arm-drag form.
- **Kytel's moonsault attempt** (logged here for session notes only — Kytel remains a guest trainer, not a trainee): he asked Jimmy to coach him through a moonsault, a move he'd never attempted. Jimmy started with an assisted version (Michael boosting him into the backflip), then switched to having Michael and Cody give him a time warp. Kytel firmly decided a moonsault was beyond his physical limitations and used it to drive home to everyone that **safety is paramount — if you don't feel you can be safe doing a move, don't do it.**
- **Heel heat discussion (before the practice matches):** Michael asked how to get initial heat when working heel for the first time. Jimmy: you have about 30 seconds at most to capture a quiet crowd, so what you do in that window matters — a reliable trick is to look at someone and tell them to shut up, even if no one's talking; it's stupid, but it works. Cody brought up the wrestler Jimmy Ray, who got heat through the dirty things he did in the ring rather than insulting fans — proof that it's not just what you say but what you do. Trash-talking the crowd is "cheap heat" and only goes so far; in-ring dirtiness is what earns long-lasting heat.
