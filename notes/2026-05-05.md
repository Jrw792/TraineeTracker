# Session: May 5, 2026

**Attendees**: Michael Wilson, Hailey Stokely, DC, Jack, Dalton, Brandon, Jeff

*Trainer: Jimmy Lee. Assistant Trainer: Cody McCulley (newly announced). Guest Trainers: Cade, Kirkland.*

## Techniques Worked
- Clothesline line drill — pulling a trainee out of the corner and delivering a clothesline (full rotation, everyone gave and received)
- Hurricanrana off the second rope (Hailey, refined with Jimmy and Kirkland; Cade then trained on taking it)
- Satisfaction (Hailey)
- Triple threat practice match — Michael, DC, Brandon
- Forearm form refinement (DC, one-on-one with Hailey after the main session)

## Notable Moments
- **Cody McCulley**: announced as a new assistant trainer.
- **Brandon**: new addition from Cody's personal roster — has been training for a while and has worked a few matches at TAW. Solid clothesline form throughout the drill, no flags.
- **Jeff**: new addition from Cody's roster, relatively new to training. Flagged early for good bump form and selling on the receiving end. First time giving clotheslines tonight — Jimmy walked him through form, he did a few practice reps before going live, then ran the exercise cleanly with no bad-form flags. Picked it up quickly.
- **Michael**: immediate compliments for notably good clothesline form. Played the heel in the triple threat. Got a tip from Jimmy on the universal "leave the ring" signal after needing Brandon to clear out fast for a cannonball.
- **Hailey**: worked with Jimmy and Kirkland on the hurricanrana off the second rope and the satisfaction — both moves she wants locked in before the next TAW show. Cade then trained on taking the hurricanrana with her and they cleaned it up nicely. Volunteered to be Dalton's corner partner during his coaching block since she's used to giving him feedback. Stayed after to coach DC on forearm form — stance and powerful presentation.
- **DC**: in the clothesline drill, kept feeding into the corner with the trainee — form improved after coaching. In the post-match psychology debrief, Jimmy flagged his forearms to Michael's back as looking weak. Stayed after for one-on-one forearm work with Hailey.
- **Jack**: got form pointers from Jimmy after his first clothesline. **Mom was present and consented to him bumping — non-bumping restriction is being lifted from his record since a parent is always present when he trains.**
- **Dalton**: rough night on the clothesline drill. First attempt flagged for hesitation and soft impact; second attempt flagged for both clothesline form and corner-pull form. Jimmy and Cody walked him through the step-by-step process for pulling someone out of the corner. First attempt after coaching was an immediate bad-form flag, followed by several more minutes of one-on-one work. With the rule that the trainee in the corner couldn't leave until pulled cleanly, Michael stayed in the corner on Dalton's first attempt. Hailey then volunteered to take the corner spot. After ~10 more minutes of coaching, Dalton's pulling form had improved enough that Jack left the corner cleanly on the first attempt.
- **Triple threat (Michael, DC, Brandon)**: clean execution from all three — no in-match flags. Post-match debrief was psychology-focused: Jimmy asked who was the heel (Michael) and pressed on why Brandon left the ring almost immediately, framing it as a planning/psychology question rather than technique. Cade reinforced the "chest and back are open game" rule with tips on maximizing both the look and the safety of those strikes. Size-differential lesson: the bigger wrestler (especially when size is part of the gimmick) has to be presented as even bigger, and the smaller wrestler has to visibly work for every move.

## General Notes
Heavy psychology night built around the triple threat debrief. Two new faces in Brandon and Jeff integrated cleanly with the existing line. Hailey was effectively co-coaching by the end — both as Dalton's corner partner during his coaching block and in the after-session forearm work with DC.
