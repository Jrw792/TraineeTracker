## Techniques Worked

- Lockup → headlock → shoulder tackle drill
- Selling drill
- Basic strikes / punch refinement
- Taking DDTs
- Giving DDTs (paired, trading)
- Drop kicks — superman (and fatboy demoed), practiced on the crash pad
- Enzuigiris — regular and step-up
- Irish whips
- Rockin' robin (tag-anyone-in wrestling)

Enzuigiri form was good apart from not enough height.

Enzuigiri came together by the end — final couple were really good and trainers were proud of the execution.

Good DDTs given and taken. Solid drop kicks (needs both feet up). Enzuigiri close — needs more leg height.

## General Notes
- Jimmy on being in a headlock: if you're hitting the person you have in the headlock, actually hit them — don't make it look weak.
- Cade gathered the group on safety and why good form matters; Cody reinforced why the repetitive drills (five and fives, bump on contact) matter — repetition drives home form, skill, and safety.
- Jimmy on taking a DDT: the most important thing is protecting your head.
- Brent and Kaleb demonstrated the superman and fatboy drop kicks; the crash pad was brought out so trainees could safely work the superman rotation.
- Brent and Kaleb demonstrated the two enzuigiri variants (regular and step-up).
- Closed with a rockin' robin (tag-anyone-in) exercise — no flags noted.
