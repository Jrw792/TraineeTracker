# Session: Tuesday, May 19, 2026

**Trainers present**: Jimmy, Cody
**Guest trainers**: Kytel, Cade Powers, Trent, Kirkland
**Attendees**: Jack, Nick, Dalton, Tyler, Hailey, Michael, Jeff

## Techniques Worked
- Lockup transitioning to a wrist lock (primarily a selling exercise)
- Criss-cross rope running with on-command drop-downs in the center of the ring
- Suplexes — giving and taking
- Body slams — giving and taking
- Flip bump refinement
- Two five-minute practice matches
- Rest spots (instruction on their use for catching breath / communicating with opponent)

## Notable Moments
- **Jack**: Didn't feel comfortable executing the suplex at first and opted to wait. The crash pad was brought out to soften the bump and remove the psychological block — he then took the suplex flawlessly, wanted to go again (second clean rep), then had the pad removed and took it fine without it. Rode the confidence wave the whole way through. Also first to volunteer for the body slam (clean). On flip bumps, good first rep (could get a touch more height); after a "straighten up" note, subsequent attempts were much better.
- **Nick**: Took the body slam with a refinement note on staying stiff and not sandbagging — took another from Kytel and executed flawlessly. As a body-slam giver, needed to mind foot placement but overall went well.
- **Tyler**: Brief hesitation on the suplex, then took it without issue. Stepped in to learn how to *give* a body slam and executed just fine.
- **Dalton**: Went over on the suplex but didn't get enough jump, so the landing was awkward — Cade had him drill the jump in suplex position and after a couple reps he took it cleanly. On flip bumps, flagged by Jimmy for dropping his shoulder too much; after coaching (and a Jimmy demonstration) his subsequent reps were much better.
- **Jeff**: On the drop-down drill, started getting back up a touch early on his first rep, took light form correction, then much better. Set up for the suplex but didn't have enough jump height — Cody had worked the form/sequence with him first, and the takeaway was that Jeff should refine flip bumps before re-attempting the suplex. On flip bumps he initially used his hands to support himself upside down; worked onto the crash pad, got cues to throw his arms down for momentum and to come in tall (he'd been leaning into the flip before getting height). Form noted as improving by the end.
- **Hailey**: Took the suplex flawlessly and the body slam without issue. Used by Jimmy to demonstrate stiff "board" body-slam form for the group, then brought in to show that size doesn't matter when giving a body slam — successfully gave Kytel a body slam after some form coaching.
- **Michael**: Used (with Cade) to demonstrate suplex form for the group. Gave Dalton a body slam with no notes on his execution.

## Practice Matches

**Michael vs. Tyler** — Opened with a lockup into a Tyler headlock, Michael reversing into wrist control, Tyler reversing to wrist control, into a short chain-wrestling spot. Built a believable failed-shoulder-tackle sequence around Michael's size (multiple failed tackles both directions before Michael finally went down and popped right back up). Headlock, whip, shoulder tackle, forearms to keep him down; corner-to-corner whip into a splash; suplex; knee to the gut; Michael fired back with punches; a failed body slam, then Tyler grabbed the head, ropes-and-back-to-center, and hit the finishing body slam. *Coaching*: Jimmy on multiple shoulder tackles — hit the ropes and re-attempt immediately rather than circling again. Cody on volume, selling, and pacing. Called a good match overall — more technical refinement than problem-correcting.

**Hailey (heel) vs. Nick** — Strong heel character work from Hailey: "match starts when I'm ready, not when you're ready," stopping the first lockup, the hand-switch fake when he reached for it, stomping his foot. Wrist lock into a headlock, whip into a shoulder tackle; Nick caught her running with a drop-down, pin, kickout at 2. Hailey grounded him, stomped the leg while taunting, picked up and slammed his foot into the mat, rolled him over and grabbed both feet — Nick kicked her off, hit three clotheslines, tripped her with a drop-down on her rope run, and rolled her up for the win.
