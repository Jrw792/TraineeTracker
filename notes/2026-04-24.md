# Session: April 24, 2026

**Attendees**: Tyler Skelton, Jack, Nick, DC, Dalton

*Guest trainers: Kytel, Kirkland. Jimmy ran the session.*

## Techniques Worked
- Pin drills — standard pin, schoolboy pin, small package pin (kick-out-and-reset format, partner rotation)
- Snap mares (Kytel and Nick each took turns giving them to the line)
- Back suplexes (DC, refinement work on the punching bag)
- Angle slams (DC)
- German suplexes (Dalton, Nick, Jack, Tyler — all on the punching bag)
- Running drop kick — DC's corner move: jump to the ropes, kick the bag, boost off into a back roll
- Drop kicks on the punching bag in the corner (Jack, with form coaching from Jimmy)
- Splashes and drop kicks on the punching bag (whole line)
- Chops — first refining form on the bag, then chopping each other for practice
- Wrestling psychology lesson + chair spot handling (slow-motion response drill from Kytel)
- Promo cutting (Jack, Nick, DC, Dalton, then Tyler — Kytel demonstrated proper form between Dalton and Tyler)

## Notable Moments
- **DC**: clean run through all three pin variations, no notes from the coaches. Drove the back suplex and angle slam refinement work himself, taking pointers from Jimmy and Kytel and drilling on the punching bag. Also introduced a running drop kick variation in the corner — leap to the ropes, kick the bag, boost off into a back roll.
- **Nick**: solid on the pin drill after a couple of small fumbles. Picked up the German suplex well after coaching. No notes on his snap mares — clean reps the whole way through.
- **Dalton**: needed Jimmy's coaching on the schoolboy pin specifically, then executed it well. Asked to learn German suplexes but explicitly didn't want to take it to a person yet — form on the bag flagged as needing significant refinement.
- **Tyler**: struggled with the small package pin because of his size — worth tracking as he develops technique to compensate. German suplex came out well with only minor form pointers.
- **Jack**: dropkick progression in the corner — coaching tweaks, second attempt better, third attempt great. Not yet strong enough to lift the bag for a German suplex; flagged as a strength-training priority.

## General Notes
Jimmy and Kytel taught the "bump card" concept — the idea that you have a finite number of safe bumps in your career, and once that card is filled, you either stop bumping or step away. Tied into a broader message about not accelerating wear-and-tear for moves that just look cool. Session closed with a circle-up where each trainee shared what kind of wrestling they want to do, and Jimmy reinforced taking care of their bodies as the foundation everything else is built on. The promo exercise to finish out was strong across the board.
